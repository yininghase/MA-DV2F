Abstract

In this paper we propose MA-DV2F: Multi-Agent Dynamic Velocity Vector Field. It is a framework for simultaneously controlling a group of vehicles in challenging environments. DV2F is generated for each vehicle independently and provides a map of reference orientation and speed that a vehicle must attain at any point on the navigation grid such that it safely reaches its target. The field is dynamically updated depending on the speed and proximity of the ego-vehicle to other agents. This dynamic adaptation of the velocity vector field allows prevention of imminent collisions. Experimental results show that MA-DV2F outperforms concurrent methods in terms of safety, computational efficiency and accuracy in reaching the target when scaling to a large number of vehicles.

Limitations

Two scenarios wherein multiple vehicles converge towards a location leading to high vehicle density. This results in so little room for navigation that the multiple vehicles cannot proceed without there being a risk of collisions. Hence, the vehicles prioritize safety and remain put in this place, leading to an impasse and failure to reach their destination.

Two scenarios wherein the targets of some vehicles are within the safety margins of some obstacles or the targets of some other vehicles. Our MA-DV2F again takes a conservative strategy, in which vehicle safety (collision avoidance) has a higher priority than target arrival. As a result, the vehicles have a preference to stay away from one another rather than driving to their respective targets.

Successful Example

In this scenario we focus on the black ego-vehicle. For all other vehicles we simulate an "unexpected behavior" by replacing the control commands predicted by MA-DV2F with uniform random noise in the range of the operating limits of the control commands, causing the neighboring vehicles to diverge away from their normal driving behavior. In some cases, this results in these vehicles colliding with the ego-vehicle, even though the ego-vehicle had taken corrective action to evade collision from them. To mitigate such collisions, a solution would be to increase the safety margin. However, as we saw in the sensitivity analysis, increasing the safety margin reduces the reach rate.
